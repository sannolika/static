Enheduanna, priestess and poet around 2250 BCE, may be the earliest author whose name we know. She wrote in Sumerian, a language now extinct. There is something sad about a vanished language: people once used it to express their thoughts, to connect to one another, and now both the speakers and their words are gone. Yet there is also a kind of optimism in the attempt to grasp what you don’t fully understand, even when the meaning evades you or becomes distorted along the way. Still, projecting your own expectations onto something unfamiliar can be misleading, even a way of exerting power, especially when interpreting the past.

In this project, I asked people to translate part of Enheduanna’s most famous work through Google Translate, each using a different source language, and then to find something meaningful in the result and record it. The voices that emerged tell a story very different from Enheduanna’s own.
